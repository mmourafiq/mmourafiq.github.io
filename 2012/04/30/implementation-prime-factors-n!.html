Finding prime factors of n! and their multiplicities

A small algorithm to compute all prime factors of n!

In this post, we will write a small algorithm to compute all prime factors of x!, given a positive integer. x The theoretical background behind the algorithm we are going to write could be found in this post.

First, we have to find all prime numbers less than x:

 1 def primes_less(x):
 2     non_prime = set()
 3     i = 2
 4     while i*i <= x:
 5         if i not in non_prime:
 6             j = i
 7             while j*i <= x:
 8                 non_prime.add(j*i)
 9                 j += 1
10         i += 1
11 
12     return set(range(2, x+1)) - non_prime

Then we can easily calculate all their multiplicities for x!:

 1 def get_primes_multiplicities(x):
 2     """
 3     Returns factors for x!
 4     """
 5     p_multi = {}  # factors multiplicities
 6     for p in primes_less(x):
 7         temp = x  
 8         mult = 0
 9         while temp!= 0:
10             temp /= p
11             mult += temp
12         p_multi[p] = mult
13     return p_multi
14 
15 def nbr_factors(x):
16     nbr = 0
17     for f in get_primes_multiplicities(x).values():
18         nbr += (f + 1)
19     return nbr

The product of the multiplicities of this prime factors is thus the possible divisors for x.
